Why Michael Jackson is the G.O.A.T

Here are 10 reasons why Michael Jackson is THE MOST influential and groundbreaking artist ever.

1. Musical Talent: An Extraordinary Gift Recognized Early

Background

Michael Jackson's extraordinary musical journey began long before he became known as the "King of Pop." Born in Gary, Indiana, on August 29, 1958, he was the eighth of ten children in the Jackson Family, where music was a part of his everyday life from the start. His mother (Katherine Jackson) was the one who discovered Michael's extraordinary natural talent in song and dance from the age of 3. She urged her husband (Joseph Jackson, Michael's father and manager) to let him join his older brothers, who were at the time known as The Jackson Brothers in 1966.

However, after recognizing his youngest son's innate talents and abilities from such an early age, he would eventually not only let Michael be a part of the Jackson Brothers, but would establish Michael as the lead singer of the band, and they would later be known as the Jackson 5.

At just five years old, Michael's remarkable sense of rhythm, natural voice ability, and confidence on stage set him apart from children of his age. While many performers spend years developing stage presence, Michael possessed an instinctive ability to connect with audiences through his voice, facial expressions, and movement. His brothers and family quickly realized that the youngest member of the group had an uncommon gift.

Development

As the lead singer of the Jackson 5, Michael performed songs that demanded vocal control, emotion, and precision beyond what most children could achieve. He learned to entertain audiences night after night, developing professional discipline while still in elementary school. His performances showcased not only a powerful singing voice but also an advanced understanding of rhythm, timing, and showmanship.

Why It Matters

A single hit song or successful album did not define Michael Jackson's musical talent. It was evident from childhood and continued to evolve throughout his career. His ability to sing, dance, write, arrange melodies, and communicate emotion through performance laid the foundation for a career and an artist who would later change music forever.

2. Dance & Performance: A Revolutionary Style That Changed Live Entertainment Forever

Background

Michael Jackson's journey as a dancer began long before he became a global icon. As the lead singer of the Jackson 5, he spent countless hours performing on stages across the United States, developing remarkable rhythm, coordination, and confidence at an age when most children were still discovering their interests. He meticulously studied legendary entertainers such as James Brown, Fred Astaire, Gene Kelly, Sammy Davis Jr., and Jackie Wilson, observing every movement, expression, and transition with precision. Rather than simply copying their performances, Michael absorbed their techniques and began creating a style that was uniquely his own.

Development

As his solo career flourished, Jackson elevated dance into an essential form of storytelling. Signature movements such as the Moonwalk, toe stand, rapid spins, isolations, and razor-sharp choreography became instantly recognizable around the world. Every performance was rehearsed with extraordinary precision, blending athletic ability, emotion, and musical interpretation into a seamless experience. His concerts became theatrical productions where dance was just as important as the songs themselves.

Why It Matters

Michael Jackson permanently transformed the expectations of live entertainment. His influence can be seen in the performances of artists who came before him from every genre, proving that choreography and stage presence could become defining elements of an artist's identity. His revolutionary approach to performance established a new standard that continues to shape concerts and popular culture decades later.

3. Songwriting & Creativity: The Visionary Mind Behind Timeless Music

Background

Although celebrated as an extraordinary performer, Michael Jackson had an equally remarkable gift for songwriting and musical creativity. From an early age, he demonstrated an exceptional ability to imagine melodies, harmonies, and rhythms long before they reached the recording studio. His curiosity and love for music encouraged him to explore multiple genres, allowing him to develop a creative perspective that extended far beyond traditional pop music.

Development

Jackson often composed songs by vocally performing every instrument, rhythm, bass line, and melody, giving producers a complete blueprint of the music he envisioned. His perfectionism became legendary, with countless hours dedicated to refining every lyric, harmony, and sound until it matched his artistic vision. By blending pop, rock, funk, soul, R&B, disco, and gospel influences, he created music that appealed to audiences across generations while remaining unmistakably his own.

Why It Matters

Michael Jackson's songwriting demonstrates that true artistry is built on imagination, innovation, and relentless dedication. Songs such as Billie Jean, Beat It, Man in the Mirror, and Earth Song continue to resonate worldwide, proving that timeless music is created through authenticity and creative excellence rather than passing trends.

4. Innovation & Music Videos: Transforming Songs into Cinematic Experiences

Background

Before Michael Jackson emerged as a solo superstar, music videos primarily served as promotional tools featuring artists performing their songs. Jackson envisioned something far more ambitious: combining music, storytelling, choreography, costumes, and filmmaking into a single artistic experience that could captivate audiences around the world.

Development

Collaborating with directors, choreographers, and filmmakers (the likes of John Landis, Martin Scorsese, Spike Lee, John Singleton, and many more), Jackson produced groundbreaking work such as Thriller, Smooth Criminal, and Remember the Time. These productions featured elaborate narratives, cinematic camera work, special effects, memorable characters, and carefully designed choreography, elevating music videos into short films that entertained millions beyond the songs themselves.

Why It Matters

Michael Jackson revolutionized visual storytelling within the music industry. His innovative approach permanently raised production standards and inspired generations of musicians, filmmakers, and creative artists to think beyond traditional performance, proving that music could become a complete cinematic experience.

5. Fashion & Style: Creating an Image That Became Instantly Iconic

Background

Michael Jackson understood that an artist's visual identity could be just as memorable as the music itself. As his career developed, he carefully crafted a distinctive style that reflected his personality, creativity, and stage presence while remaining instantly recognizable.

Development

Working alongside talented designers and costume creators, Jackson introduced legendary fashion elements, including the single sequined glove, black fedora, military-inspired jackets, and loafers paired with white socks, and the iconic red leather jackets from Thriller and Beat It, and character fits from the Smooth Criminal, and Billie Jean music videos. These pieces became inseparable from his performances and helped establish one of the most recognizable images in entertainment history.

Why It Matters

Michael Jackson demonstrated that fashion could become an extension of artistic expression rather than simple clothing. His influence continues to inspire designers, performers, and fans worldwide, with many of his signature looks remaining cultural symbols decades after their debut.

6. Storytelling & Visual Art: Building Worlds Through Music, Dance, and Film

Background

Michael Jackson believed that music should be experienced both visually and emotionally. Rather than simply recording songs, he sought to create complete artistic worlds that immersed audiences in stories filled with imagination, symbolism, and cinematic detail.

Development

Through elaborate music videos, choreography, costumes, and visual effects, Jackson blended music, dance, theater, and film into unified artistic experiences. Productions such as Thriller, Ghosts, and Remember the Time demonstrated his ability to transform entertainment into compelling visual narratives that captivated audiences around the globe.

Why It Matters

His approach expanded the possibilities of artistic expression and inspired musicians and filmmakers to view music as a storytelling medium. By merging multiple art forms into one experience, Michael Jackson forever changed the relationship between music and visual culture.

7. Global Influence: A Cultural Phenomenon That United the World

Background

From the earliest stages of his solo career, Michael Jackson demonstrated a rare ability to connect with audiences regrdless of language, nationality, or culture. His music crossed geographical boundaries and reached listeners from virtually every corner of the world, creating a shared experience that transcended differences.

Development

Through record-breaking tours, chart-topping albums, and unforgettable performances, Jackson became one of the most recognizable figures in modern history. He also helped break racial barriers within mainstream music television and expanded opportunities for future generations of artists by proving that exceptional talent could unite audiences worldwide.

Why It Matters

Michael Jackson evolved beyond being a musician to become a true global cultural icon. His influence continues to inspire singers, dancers, athletes, actors, and creators worldwide, demonstrating the universal power of music and artistic expression.

8. Humanitarian Contributions: Using Music and Influence to Inspire Positive Change

Background

Beyond his achievements as an entertainer, Michael Jackson believed that artists carried a responsibility to improve the lives of others. Throughout his career, he consistently supported charitable organizations and children's hospitals

Development

Jackson participated in fundraising efforts, visited hospitals, donated millions of dollars to charitable organizations, and recorded songs such as Man in the Mirror, Heal the World, and Earth Song, encouraging compassion, unity, and responsibility toward others and the planet.

Why It Matters

His humanitarian work demonstrated that music could inspire both emotion and action. By using his platform to promote hope and positive change, Michael Jackson created a legacy that extends beyond entertainment and continues to encourage acts of kindness and service around the world.

9. Records & Achievements: Milestones That Redefined Success

Background

From childhood through adulthood, Michael Jackson consistently achieved levels of commercial and critical success rarely witnessed in the entertainment industry. His dedication to excellence translated into accomplishments that reshaped the standards by which artists are measured.

Development

His career produced the best-selling album in history, record-breaking world tours, numerous Grammy Awards, hundreds of millions of records sold, and countless chart achievements spanning multiple decades. Each milestone reflected not only popularity but also extraordinary artistic consistency and global appeal.

Why It Matters

While statistics alone cannot define greatness, Michael Jackson's achievements provide measurable evidence of his unprecedented impact on music and popular culture. His records continue to serve as benchmarks for artists aspiring to reach the highest levels of success.

10. Lasting Legacy: An Enduring Standard of Artistic Excellence

Background

Even years after his passing, Michael Jackson's influence remains deeply woven into music, dance, fashion, and entertainment. New generations continue discovering his work, ensuring that his artistry remains relevant across changing eras.

Development

Modern performers regularly study his choreography, vocal techniques, songwriting, production methods, and stagecraft. His music continues to be streamed, performed, sampled, and celebrated worldwide, while his signature dance moves and visual style remain instantly recognizable.

Why It Matters

Michael Jackson's legacy extends far beyond awards and record sales. He transformed the way artists create, perform, and connect with audiences, leaving an enduring influence that contimnues to shape popular culture. His combination of talent, innovation, creativity, and global impact is why millions continue to recognize him as the greatest entertainer and artist of all time.
